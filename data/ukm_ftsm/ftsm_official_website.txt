Faculty Background
The Faculty of Information Science and Technology (Fakulti Teknologi dan Sains Maklumat) (FTSM) has established its academic roots as early as the conception of Universiti Kebangsaan Malaysia (UKM). Before FTSM ever became a full-fledged faculty of its own, courses related to Computer Science have long been offered by UKM since the university was established in 1970. In line with the emergence of the computer science discipline, UKM set up the Statistics and Computer Science Unit in 1977 under the Quantitative School. The Statistics and Computer Science Unit was upgraded to become the Computer Science Department in 1982 while the Quantitative School became the Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science in 1991. Academic programs at the Undergraduate, Master and Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) levels have been introduced incrementally. The number of academic staff with PhDs increased in accordance with the national requirements related to enhancing the quality of teaching and research.
In the 1990s, the world economy was driven by knowledge-based economics which was in tandem with the emergence of Information and Communication Technology (ICT). Not to be left out of these exciting developments, Malaysia devised various policies and initiatives including the Multimedia Super Corridor(MSC). Subsequently, ICT has become one of the nation’s most important industries. In line with the changes that have moved the world from the industrial to the information era, the Computer Science Department was upgraded on 1 October 1994 to become the Faculty of Information Science and Technology (Fakulti Teknologi dan Sains Maklumat) (FTSM). Strengthened by 40 academic staff, the faculty was awarded the ‘MSC company’ status on 24th November 2000.
Today, the FTSM family consists of a staff of 138 members. Academics, research and consultancy activities are carried out by 90 faculty members that includes 8 professors, 26 associate professors, 48 senior lecturers, 2 lecturers and 6 IT Instructors. The Faculty is also managed with the support of 25 administrative staff and 23 ICT support staff. The FTSM campus is located at the UKM Main Campus in Bangi. It also houses 3 research centers which are driven by experts in various fields of research. Since its inception, FTSM has progressed rapidly in various aspects. FTSM students too, are making their presence felt by being actively involved in numerous programs and competitions at the national and international levels.
At present, research in FTSM is streamlined into 3 focus areas for innovation, guided by experts at the 3 research centers hosted within FTSM: the Center for Artificial Intelligence Technology (CAIT), the Center for Cyber Security (CYBER), and the Center for Software Technology and Management (SOFTAM). Between 2019 and 2022, these 3 research centers collectively managed RM18,029,703.30 in research grants and funding and have collectively produced 154 innovations, published 644 journal articles and 47 books within these 3 focus areas.
In recognition of its many academic and research achievements since its establishment, FTSM was awarded the highly coveted status of “Premier Digital Tech Institution” by the Malaysian Digital Economy Corporation (MDEC) on 18 December 2023.

Faculty Map
The Faculty of Information Science and Technology (FTSM) is located near to the Faculty of Engineering and Built Environment in Bangi Campus, Selangor. You may come here via the First Link entrance by using the Kuala Lumpur – Seremban Highway and exiting through the Kajang toll, or may also exit through the Bandar Baru Bangi toll. For those who are coming from Sepang, you may take the Second Link entrance and head directly to FTSM. Among the public transportation available to for your convenience are:
1.	KTM Komuter - towards Seremban. Kindly depart at UKM Station. Take a taxi or a RapidKL Bus No.430 to FTSM
2.	RapidKL Bus – take the No.E2 bus to Kajang Terminal. Once arrive, switch to RapidKL Bus No. 430 to FTSM
3.	KLIA Limousine Service – from Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA)
4.	KLIA Express Transit – stop at Bandar Tasik Selatan Station, take the KTM Komuter towards Seremban and stop at UKM Station. Then, take a taxi or RapidKL Bus No.430 to FTSM

The Faculty of Information Science and Information Technology is a stimulating environment in which new ideas, experimental designs, and robust systems are plentiful.
Our teaching programmes are always among the top in Malaysia. We also enjoy strong links with industries, which helps to ensure that our research and teaching is well informed and relevant to the needs of society.
Life for undergraduate majors centers around the various educational computing facility which is housed in the faculty. Apart from the major academic courses, all undergraduate students will participate in various Special Interest Groups (SIGs), aiming to promote students in applying and fostering their skills dan knowledge to industries and communities.
These SIGs ranging from Intelligent Machines Club, Cyber Hack & Ethics to Video Innovation inculcate creativities and innovations of our students. In the final year of the four years of the undergraduate programs, students will embark on a final year project and five months of industrial attachment.
Life for postgraduate students will mainly focus on the three research centers within the faculty: the Center for Artificial Intelligence (CAIT), the Center for Software Technology & Management (SOFTAM), and the Center for Cyber Security (Siber). Students will belong to either of these research centers which support conducive and supportive research environments.

Final Year Project
The course trains students to conduct a project individually. Through this project, students are able to integrate knowledge and skills acquired during the study for solving a specific problem. This course requires students to prepare project documentation such as proposal/project plan, requirements specification, design specification and test specification. Based on the documents, students need to develop a computing product by using the appropriate methods, techniques and tools within a stipulated time frame. The students later have to present the documents and product as project deliverables to the designated stakeholders. The deliverables shall demonstrate sufficient level of complexity with respect to the chosen computing field.
OBJECTIVE
Through the project, students aim to achieve the following objectives:
To integrate existing and new knowledge related to computing fields.
To develop a computing product based on the proposed methods and techniques.
To present the developed computing product verbally and in writing.
To produce a computing product that possesses entrepreneurial values.
To practise responsible, ethical and professional attitudes.

Student Mobility & Exchange Program
Student mobility is one of UKM's and FTSM’s strategic priorities. Student mobility or exchange programs offer students the chance to study or conduct research at partner institutions abroad, enhancing students' academic and cultural experiences. These programs enable students to spend a semester or academic year at a partner university, where they can take courses that count towards their degree at UKM or work on research projects. Mobility and exchange programs are coordinated by the Office of International Relations Center with collaboration from various UKM faculties and institutes. For general information on the mobility programs at UKM, please visit: www.ukm.my/pha
Embarking on a mobility program is a gateway to an extraordinary adventure that can redefine your academic and personal journey. Immerse yourself in a new culture, gain fresh perspectives from diverse teaching methods, and access unique courses that broaden your academic experience in ways your home institution may not offer. This experience goes beyond traditional learning; it cultivates essential life skills such as adaptability, independence, and cross-cultural communication. Engaging with a global network of peers and professionals enhances your resume and deepens your understanding of the world, making you more prepared to thrive in a dynamic, interconnected global landscape. Embrace the opportunity to transform your education and expand your world your future self will thank you for it!

OVERVIEW
PROGRAMME STRUCTURE
Computer Science is the scientific and practical approach to computation and its applications. Computer science is a fast-moving field that brings together many disciplines, including mathematics, programming, engineering, the natural sciences, and linguistics. We offer a flexible programme with strong fundamentals of computer science that provides students the required skills to solve problems and to create future technology as a computer scientist. The programme is divided into four (4) tracks Software Technology, Network Technology, Applied Data Science, and Intelligent Machine which the students will be able to choose in their second year. Apart from the 12-weeks industrial training in the fourth year, the programme is designed to be in line with industrial requirements through our industrial partners such as IBM, CISCO, and Skymind.
